Hi, I'm Pedro M. A. Fernandes

I'm a game developer and scientific researcher from Almada, Portugal, and I've worked on projects as a Game/Level Designer, Q/A Tester and Sound Designer.

The Sixth Sun

The Sixth Sun is a retro, 3D first-person shooter video game that is set in the times of the Aztec civilization, where the player takes the role of a warrior chosen by the gods to fight an alien invasion. The project was developed for approximately 10 months, until the release of a vertical slice demonstration in July of 2021.

My main roles in the project were as Game & Level Designer and 3D Modeller.

Orbiter

Orbiter is a joint project with CICANT made in Unity/C# with the purpose of promoting inclusivity for people with physical and mental limitations by providing an accessible game and interface.

The game has the player jump through orbits while dodging comets and alien ships in order to reach the wormhole at the center, progressing to the next level. The interface, was a custom-built 2-button arduino interface, who's assembly and programming was also responsability of the team.

[Scientific Article] SimpAI: Evolutionary Heuristics for the ColorShapeLinks Board Game Competition

In Videojogos 2020 - the 12th Internacional Conference on Videogame Sciences and Arts, I, along with the Co-Authors Pedro M. A. Inácio, Hugo Feliciano and Nuno Fachada, presented the peer-reviewed scientific paper "SimpAI: Evolutionary Heuristics for the ColorShapeLinks Board Game Competition".

In this article, we explored the application of evolutionary algorithms for the development of an artificial agent set to play Simplexity in the ColorShapeLinks AI competition

Learning engagement in a pre-alpha version of an educational game: evaluation and proposed solutions

In the 8th International Congress of Audiovisual Researchers, I, along with Lília Marcelino, Rafael C. e Silva, Pedro Inácio, João Cerqueira, and Inácio Amerio, presented the communication: "Learning engagement in a pre-alpha version of an educational game: evaluation and proposed solutions".

Here, we showed our analysis of the results of several play-testing sessions of a prototype build for an educational game titled Space Adventure. I was not involved in the development of the game itself.
